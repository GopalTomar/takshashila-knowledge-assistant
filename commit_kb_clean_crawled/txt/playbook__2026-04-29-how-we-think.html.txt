How We Think — Critical Thinking Norms
playbook
The frameworks and principles Takshashila staff use to reason rigorously about policy questions.
Published
April 29, 2026
TL;DR
: Takshashila's thinking norms: come with three alternatives not just a problem, lead with conclusions, quantify claims, hold hypotheses lightly, and apply Occam's Razor.
Three Cardinal Rules
Prepare before seeking help
— come with at least three pre-considered options, not just a problem statement
Solution-oriented communication
— accompany any publicly raised issue with proposed solutions
Action over permission
— better to apologise for acting than to wait indefinitely for approval
Baloney Detection (Michael Shermer)
Before asserting a claim, check:
Is the source reliable and independent?
Has the claim been verified independently?
Does it align with what is otherwise known?
Does the evidence support this claim over alternatives?
Sagan's Method
Seek independent confirmation of facts
Encourage debate among experts — do not appeal to authority
Test multiple hypotheses simultaneously
Quantify
— if something can be measured, measure it; it allows better discrimination between competing ideas
Do not become attached to your own hypothesis
Every link in an argument chain must be sound
Apply Occam's Razor: prefer the simpler explanation when evidence is equal
Hypotheses must be falsifiable — unfalsifiable claims are not scientific
The Reverse Bollywood Format
In communication and presentations:
lead with the conclusion
. State the key point or recommendation in 60 seconds. Methodology and evidence follow. Never build up to a reveal.
