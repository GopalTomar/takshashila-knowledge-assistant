Tankscrub — Philosophy & Format
playbook
What Tankscrub is, why it exists, and how to participate well.
Published
April 29, 2026
TL;DR
: Tankscrub is a Thursday morning gathering for half-baked ideas and organisational curiosity — deliberately informal, self-deprecating in tone, and the opposite of a formal presentation.
Key Points
Informal by design — resist the urge to make it a polished presentation
Share preliminary thoughts with intellectual rigour but without unnecessary formality
The tone should be irreverent and self-deprecating
Keeps the team's curiosity and learning radars active
The Philosophy (Nitin Pai)
"Our work is too serious for seriousness."
Tankscrub exists to keep oldtimers on their toes and ensure curiosity never stagnates. The common pitfall is treating it as a formal presentation venue — don't.
How to Start a Tankscrub Discussion
Three suggested conversation openers:
"A few days ago, I was reading X and it struck me that Y"
"D says X, and I think Y evidence supports it, but I think Z"
"The Z government's recent proposal P can lead to C consequences"
Thursday Thali
One person or group arranges monthly lunch for the office (~₹250 per plate). Develops negotiation skills and camaraderie. The SOP: select cuisine → confirm logistics → announce preferences → collect counts → coordinate delivery → submit for reimbursement.
