The Flag System — Gold, Red, Yellow, White
playbook
How Takshashila uses four flags to signal achievements, blockers, and accountability in day-to-day work.
Published
April 29, 2026
TL;DR
: Four flags — Gold (win), Red (urgent blocker), Yellow (stuck after asking), White (owning a mistake) — are the shared language for signalling work status at Takshashila.
Key Points
Flags are raised in meetings and on Mattermost, not just kept private
Gold flags feed into stakeholder reports and performance reviews — raise them
Don't wait for a meeting to raise a Red flag; do it immediately
White flags are encouraged, not penalised — mistakes are steps toward growth
The Four Flags
🟡 Gold Flag — Achievements
Claim when an achievement is unlocked or when you want to share something that made you happy or proud.
Use for: publications, successful events, closed grants, positive stakeholder feedback, course completions.
Also feeds into stakeholder reporting and performance reviews — log them.
🔴 Red Flag — Urgent Blocker
Raise when your progress is blocked, there is a crisis, and you need immediate help and attention from colleagues.
Do not wait for the next meeting.
Post on Mattermost right away.
🟠 Yellow Flag — Persistent Blocker
Raise when you have already asked for help and are still stuck. Escalates the red flag to the whole team.
🏳️ White Flag — Owning a Mistake
Raise to own up to mistakes and oversights.
The organisation actively encourages white flags. Surfacing mistakes quickly allows collective course-correction. Hiding them does not.
