Do Not Resolve or Delete Comments When Sharing Docs for Review
playbook
Deleting reviewer comments frustrates collaborators — always respond to comments with your reasoning instead of resolving or removing them.
Published
June 16, 2026
TL;DR
: Deleting reviewer comments frustrates collaborators — always respond to comments with your reasoning instead of resolving or removing them.
Key Points
Never resolve or delete comments on a Google Doc sent for review.
Respond to each comment with the action taken — whether the change was made or why it was not.
Deleting reviewer comments is considered disrespectful and frustrating to collaborators.
This practice maintains a clear audit trail of feedback and editorial decisions.
Context
At Takshashila, research documents are frequently shared internally for peer review before publication. This norm was flagged to ensure that all researchers and writers follow a consistent, respectful commenting etiquette on collaborative documents, particularly Google Docs.
