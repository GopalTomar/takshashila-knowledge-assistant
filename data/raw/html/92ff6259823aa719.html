West Asian War Daily Bulletin – March 27, 2026 | Day 28 of Operation Epic Fury

Iran permits ships from China, Russia, India, Iraq, and Pakistan to transit the Strait of Hormuz. Trump extends his deadline for Iran to April 6. US delivers 15-point ceasefire framework to Iran via Pakistan; Iran rejects it and issues a five-point counteroffer. Israel strikes Arak heavy water complex and Ardakan uranium plant — IAEA calls for maximum restraint. IDF Chief warns of institutional collapse risk.

March 27, 2026

Iran
West Asia

Authors

West Asia Desk

Today’s Key Developments

This space provides a daily brief tracking key developments in the West Asian conflict.

India

Foreign Policy & Neighbourhood

  • The Indian government formed an inter-ministerial group headed by Defence Minister Rajnath Singh to monitor the West Asia conflict and its implications for India, with specific focus on natural gas and crude oil supply concerns.
  • Prime Minister Modi chaired a virtual meeting of chief ministers and lieutenant governors to review India’s energy preparedness in light of the ongoing conflict, calling upon states to mount a collective response comparable to India’s handling of the COVID-19 pandemic. He expressed optimism that “Team India will overcome this situation.”

Economy

  • Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman stated that diesel and petrol rates remain unchanged despite the spike in global crude oil prices caused by the conflict, adding that the government is “active and responding to the evolving situation.”

Geopolitics

Strait of Hormuz

  • Iran’s Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi announced that ships from five nations — China, Russia, India, Iraq, and Pakistan — would be permitted to transit the Strait of Hormuz.
  • Iran also agreed to a UN request to allow humanitarian aid and agricultural and fertiliser shipments through the strait, in recognition of the disruption to the global spring planting season. UN Secretary-General António Guterres warned that the prolonged closure was “choking the movement of oil, gas and fertilizer at a critical moment in the global planting season.”
  • The IRGC simultaneously warned that shipping to and from ports of “allies and supporters of the Israeli-American enemies” remains prohibited and will face “harsh measures.” Iran also briefly blocked two Chinese ships from entering the strait before later clarifying their permitted status.
  • Iranian state media reported that Iran’s Parliament is planning to formalise fees for all ships passing through the Strait of Hormuz.

Pakistan

  • Pakistani Foreign Minister Ishaq Dar announced that Iran has agreed to allow 20 ships under the Pakistani flag to transit the strait, with two ships crossing daily. Dar called it “a welcome and constructive gesture by Iran” and “a harbinger of peace.”
  • Turkey said talks involving Pakistan, Saudi Arabia, and Egypt could take place in Pakistan this weekend, as Islamabad continues its mediation between the US and Iran.

China

  • Two Chinese vessels were initially blocked by Iran at the Strait of Hormuz before Iran’s subsequent announcement permitted Chinese-flagged ships to pass. China has continued to purchase Iranian crude throughout the conflict.

The United States

  • US President Trump extended his self-imposed deadline for Iran to open the Strait of Hormuz by a further 10 days, to April 6, 2026.
  • US Special Envoy Steve Witkoff confirmed at a Cabinet meeting that Washington has delivered a 15-point action list to Iran as the framework for a ceasefire, via Pakistan as intermediary. The proposal includes Iran committing to never pursue nuclear weapons, dismantling existing nuclear capabilities, and reopening the Strait of Hormuz.
  • Iran rejected the US proposal and presented a five-point counteroffer via state media, demanding: a complete halt to attacks, concrete guarantees against future war, payment of war reparations, and recognition of Iranian sovereignty over the Strait of Hormuz.

Global Systems

Energy & Supply Chains

  • Brent crude rose to approximately $112–114 per barrel on March 27, reversing the earlier decline, after ceasefire talks produced no breakthrough and Iran maintained its chokehold on the strait.
  • Iran’s Parliament began formal proceedings to legislate fees for ships transiting the Strait of Hormuz.

Diaspora

  • The number of Indians who have returned to India since February 28 has crossed 3,75,000, including approximately 1,000 from Iran alone, of whom over 700 are medical students.

Defence & Security

Weapons Systems & Defence Tech

  • Saudi Arabia’s Defence Ministry reported intercepting and destroying two drones aimed at Riyadh on March 27.
  • An Iranian missile and drone strike on Prince Sultan Air Base in Saudi Arabia, which hosts US troops, injured at least 15 American service members — including five critically — and damaged a US refuelling aircraft. It was the second such strike on the base within the same week; a prior attack injured 14 troops, bringing total wounded at the base to more than two dozen in the past week.
  • Israel struck the Shahid Khondab Heavy Water Complex in Arak, historically used in uranium enrichment testing, and the Ardakan yellowcake uranium production plant in Yazd Province. The Israeli military described the Ardakan strike as “a major blow to Iran’s nuclear program.” The IAEA reiterated its call for maximum restraint following the strikes on nuclear-related facilities.
  • Israel also struck what it described as Iran’s primary facility for the production of missiles and sea mines in Yazd.
  • Iran’s Foreign Minister Araghchi confirmed that Israel targeted Iran’s two largest steel factories, a power plant, and civilian nuclear sites, and warned of “heavy” retaliation. Iran warned it will “no longer be an eye for an eye” following attacks on its infrastructure.

War Tactics & Strategy

  • Despite Trump’s extended deadline, Israel pressed ahead with new strikes on Iran, targeting sites in Tehran and in the Kermanshah and Dezful areas. Israeli Defence Minister Israel Katz publicly vowed to “escalate and expand” attacks on Iran, saying, “Despite the warnings, the firing continues.”
  • IDF Chief of Staff Lt. Gen. Eyal Zamir warned government ministers that the IDF risks being stretched to the point of institutional collapse due to mounting manpower shortages and expanded operational demands across Lebanon and Iran simultaneously.
  • Iran, a day after the US and Defence Secretary Hegseth announced the “neutralization” of Iran’s military, demonstrated continued capacity with the Prince Sultan Air Base strike — a direct rebuttal to claims of neutralisation.
